Morning Hacks for NYUAD Students

Start Your Day with Movement

Starting your day with some physical activity can boost your mood and energy levels. Whether it's a jog around campus or stretching exercises, a little movement can help you feel more awake and focused.

Change Up Your Routine

Breaking away from a rigid routine can provide a refreshing change. Even small adjustments, like switching up your morning activities or taking a different route to class, can inject excitement into your day.

Morning Journaling

Journaling in the morning can clear your mind and set a positive intention for the day. It helps you reflect, express your emotions, and clarify your goals, ensuring a productive and mindful start to your day.

Plan Something Fun for After Class

Having something to look forward to after class, like a social meetup or a personal activity, can create anticipation and break the cycle of routine. It helps you stay motivated and make the most of your time at NYUAD.

Listen to a Podcast or Playlist

Listening to a podcast or playlist in the morning can provide inspiration, learning, or entertainment, depending on your mood. Choose something that stimulates your mind or boosts your energy as you start your day.

Meet Our Outstanding Alumni

These remarkable young women have embarked on a journey of excellence, proving that determination and passion lead to success.

Producing Work, Staying Healthy, and Developing New Habits

Maryam Alshehhi, a literature and creative writing and political science student, sought experiences that challenged her. After transferring to NYUAD, she embraced opportunities like an internship at TwoFour54, where she worked with Tent Pictures Productions.

During her internship, she led the creation of a "making-of" book for the film Cats Away, which documents old Abu Dhabi through street cats. Through interviews and film analysis, she contributed to what could be one of the first such books in the UAE.

Beyond work, she stepped out of her comfort zone by studying French, maintaining a daily gym routine, attending yoga classes, and exploring Abu Dhabi. Her structured approach helped her balance professional and personal growth.

From Self-Doubt to a Job at McKinsey

Alia ElKattan, Class of 2020, graduated with a degree in computer science and minors in political science, sound and music computing, and interactive media. Initially undecided, she explored various fields before settling on computer science.

During her sophomore year, she secured an internship as a UX designer at McKinsey & Company, despite feeling unqualified. That experience later led to a full-time job as a Junior Digital Analyst.

ElKattan’s advice to students is to trust the process. “There’s so much pressure to figure out what you want to do forever, but what really matters is finding something you enjoy.”
